1. Does our current set of physical laws predict consciousness, or are we missing laws?
  2. What is the smallest collection of atoms that can be conscious?
  3. Suppose you have someone whose two brain halves are completely separate. Is each half conscious?1 What degree of connectivity do you need between the two halves to get one consciousness instead of two? Why isnt any contiguous subset of neurons conscious?
  4. What conditions are required for continuity of consciousness? Will your consciousness continue if you suffer amnesia? A concussion? Wake up from a coma? What about if you atomize and then reconstruct your brain?
  5. Can consciousness and/or continuity of consciousness be examined experimentally?
  1. 1Note that despite all the intriguing experimental results, split-brain patients only exhibit a single will, so presumably they have only a single consciousness.
