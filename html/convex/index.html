1.2. Topology

Interior Point
A point such that a centered ball of some sufficiently small radius epsilon fits entirely within .
Relative Interior Point
An slight refinement of an interior point. Instead of saying all points in the ball must fit within , you say all points in the ball that are also on the affine hull of must fit within . This lets you define interiors for subspaces.
Boundary Point
A point such that a centered ball always intersects and s complement. Alternatively, a point such that all centered balls of radius less than some threshold intersects and s complement.
